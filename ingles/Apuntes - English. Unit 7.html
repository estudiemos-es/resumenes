Apuntes - English. Unit 7

Unidad 7. Pedir y dar opiniones: reseñas

Introducción y contextualización

El ser humano se distingue por el uso de la razón para expresar su opinión. La comunicación permite transmitir ideas mediante el lenguaje verbal y no verbal. Esta unidad se enfoca en aprender a expresar opiniones a través de la oralidad y la escritura, reconociendo que las expresiones pueden variar culturalmente.

Glosario

Listening

Speaking

La expresión oral de opiniones es un componente clave de esta unidad. Se practica dando una opinión sobre una experiencia reciente, como la visita a un restaurante.

A continuación, se presenta una tabla con expresiones comunes para:

  • Gustos
  • Positivo: I like, I love, I prefer, I enjoy, I'd rather, I'm very fond of
  • Negativo: I hate, I dislike, I don't like, I'm not really into, I'm not interested in, I'm not very fond of
  • Opiniones
  • I think, I believe, From my point of view, I consider, In my opinion, I'm sure that
  • Acuerdo
  • Positivo: I agree, You are right, I see your point, I completely share your opinion, Absolutely, Definitely
  • Negativo: I disagree, You are partly right, but…, I don’t see your point, I don’t share your opinion, I think you are wrong, You may be right, but…

Ejemplo de opinión personal:

Se presenta un ejemplo de cómo expresar una opinión sobre un restaurante: "I went to a new restaurant this week. At the beginning, I thought it wasn’t going to be anything special, but then it surprised me. From my point of view, it was really beautiful. In addition, the menu was rather complete. My family had different kinds of dishes and all of them were quite delicious. I really recommend this restaurant to everyone, as it is a relaxed place where you can have tasty food enjoying the company and the place."

Reading

Writing

La escritura de reseñas es una habilidad importante, tanto para darlas como para comprender las que se reciben sobre un lugar o evento, incluso sobre el propio centro de trabajo.

Ejemplo de reseña:

Se proporciona un ejemplo de una reseña sobre una clínica de belleza: "I recently visited this beauty clinic and had a great experience. The staff was friendly and professional, making me feel comfortable throughout my treatment. The clinic was clean and well-maintained, which added to the overall positive atmosphere. The treatment I received was effective and I could see noticeable results afterwards. The prices were reasonable compared to other clinics in the area, making it a good value for the quality of service provided. I would definitely recommend this beauty clinic to anyone looking for professional and effective aesthetic treatments in a welcoming environment. Overall, I had a great experience and will be returning for future treatments."

Mediation

En esta sección, se practica la comprensión y transformación de opiniones a diferentes formatos.

Ejemplo de opinión:

Se presenta la opinión sobre un concierto: "Last night I went to a concert of my favorite band and it was an amazing experience! The atmosphere was electric and the band’s performance was top-notch. They played all their hits and had the crowd singing along the whole time. The sound quality was great and the light show added to the overall excitement. However, there were a few downsides to the concert. The venue was quite crowded, making it hard to move around or find a good spot to watch the band. Also, the drinks were overpriced and the lines at the bar were way too long. It would have been nice if they had more staff working to serve everyone in a timely manner. Overall, despite the minor inconveniences, I had a fantastic time at the concert and would definitely go see this band again in the future."

Grammar support

Esta sección se centra en el Present Perfect (presente perfecto).

  • Definición: Se utiliza para expresar acciones que comenzaron en el pasado y continúan hasta el presente, o cuyas consecuencias son relevantes en el presente. Los tiempos "perfectos" se refieren a acciones completas.
  • Estructura afirmativa:
  • Sujeto + auxiliar (have/has) + verbo principal en participio pasado.
  • El auxiliar "have" se usa con I, you, we, they. "Has" se usa con he, she, it.
  • El participio pasado puede ser regular (añadiendo -ed o -d al verbo base) o irregular (requiere memorización).
  • Ejemplo: "It has rained today."
  • Estructura negativa:
  • Sujeto + auxiliar en forma negativa (haven't/hasn't) + verbo principal en participio pasado.
  • Ejemplo: "It hasn’t rained today."
  • Estructura interrogativa:
  • Auxiliar (have/has) + sujeto + verbo principal en participio pasado + ?
  • Ejemplo: "Has it rained today?"
  • Expresiones típicas:
  • For: Indica duración.
  • "It has rained for two hours." (Ha llovido durante dos horas).
  • Since: Indica el punto de inicio de la acción.
  • "It has rained since ten o’clock." (Ha llovido desde las diez de la mañana).
  • Just: Indica que la acción acaba de ocurrir.
  • "It has just rained." (Acaba de llover).
  • Already: Indica que la acción ya ha ocurrido.
  • "It has already rained." (Ya ha llovido).
  • Yet: Se usa en negativas e interrogativas para preguntar si algo ha ocurrido o indicar que no ha ocurrido "todavía".
  • "It hasn’t rained yet." (No ha llovido todavía).
