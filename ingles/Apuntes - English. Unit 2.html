Unidad 2. Pedir y dar información personal

1. Introducción y contextualización

Cuando conocemos a alguien por primera vez, es fundamental saber cómo pedir y dar información personal básica. Esto incluye detalles como el nombre, la edad, la procedencia o las aficiones. Esta habilidad es útil tanto en el ámbito personal como profesional, ya sea para registrar datos de nuevas personas o para presentarnos a nosotros mismos. Dominar estas interacciones comunicativas es esencial para establecer contactos y trabajar eficazmente con otros.

4. Expresión oral (speaking)

La expresión oral es clave para establecer un primer contacto. Aquí se presenta una lista de preguntas comunes y sus posibles respuestas para pedir y dar información personal, junto con un diálogo de ejemplo.

Preguntas y respuestas comunes:

  • What’s your name?
  • My name is… / I am…
  • What’s your surname / family name?
  • My surname / family name is…
  • How old are you?
  • I am ____ (years old).
  • Where are you from? / Where do you come from? / Where do you live?
  • I’m from… / I come from… / I live in…
  • Who do you live with?
  • I live with…
  • What do you like doing in your free time?
  • I like / love ______
  • Do you study or do you work?
  • I study _______ / I work ______
  • What’s your phone number?
  • My phone number is ___________
  • What’s your email address?
  • My email address is ___________

Ejemplo de diálogo:

A. Hello!

B. Hi

A. What’s your name?

B. My name is Julia. And yours?

A. I’m Paul. Nice to meet you.

B. You too. Where do you come from, Paul?

A. I come from Poland. Where are you from?

B. I’m from Italy. I am twenty years old, and you? How old are you?

A. I’m twenty one. Do you study or do you work?

B. I’m working as a teacher in a kindergarten, and you?

A. That’s nice! I am studying vocational training to be a teacher too!

B. That sounds great. What’s your phone number? I could call you if you need some help.

A. My phone number is 56780023145532. Thank you so much! What’s your phone number? I will call you if necessary.

B. Mine is 2563413396024. It was great to meet you. Have a nice day!

A. You too! See you soon.

6. Expresión escrita (writing)

Basándose en la unidad anterior sobre presentaciones y despedidas, esta sección se enfoca en ampliar esa estructura para incluir la solicitud y el suministro de información personal básica, utilizando el vocabulario y las estructuras aprendidas.

Ejemplo de diálogo escrito:

A. Good morning, how are you?

B. Hi! I’m fine, thanks! And you?

A. Great, thanks! What’s your name?

B. My name is Sue, and yours?

A. I’m Tom. Nice to meet you!

B. You too. How old are you?

A. I am eighteen years old, and you?

B. I’m nineteen. Where do you live?

A. I live in Madrid, and you? Where are you from?

B. I’m from Italy but I live in a small town close to Madrid. I live alone and I would like to live with a flatmate.

A. Really? We were looking for a flatmate, too! You could live with us.

B. Who do you live with?

A. I live with two more flatmates, they are really nice.

B. That sounds great. What’s your phone number? I will call you today.

A. My phone number is 526770312256.

B. Great, I will call you later. Thank you so much! Have a nice day!

A. Perfect. You too! It was great to meet you.

7. Mediación

La mediación implica extraer información personal de un texto y responder preguntas sobre ella.

Fragmento de texto:

"I’m John Smith. I live in Torremolinos, although I come from the United Kingdom. I’m twenty and I live in Spain because I wanted to work here for a short period of time. I am working as a waiter, although I am studying in order to be a dentist. I am living with three flatmates right now, and I miss my family very much. They live in London. Gary is my dad, Lucy is my mum and Anne, my sister. I also have a dog, with which I love going out for a walk. I also like having pizza on Fridays with my friends."

Respuestas a las preguntas (como John Smith):

1. What’s your name?

  • My name is John.

2. What’s your family name?

  • My family name is Smith.

3. How old are you?

  • I am twenty years old.

4. Where do you live?

  • I live in Torremolinos, Spain.

5. Who do you live with?

  • I live with three flatmates.

6. Where do you come from?

  • I come from the United Kingdom.

7. Do you study or do you work?

  • I work as a waiter, and I am studying to be a dentist.
